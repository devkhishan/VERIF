INTRODUCTION


These days it is important to verify a person’s identity before letting them do anything in public.

And in countries like India the most widely used method of verification of a person’s identity is “Aadhar card” which has every bit of your information from name to address, which is very crucial information, when it ends up in the wrong person's hand could be a disaster.


PROBLEM


We are sometimes even asked to give a copy of our aadhar card to non-government organizations to prove our identity which is a big danger.


  • Privacy of the user is affected (by giving his info on aadhar card to a third person)

  • This information could be misused by others

  • Also, the centralization of data will be a problem if there are any compromises in security of those data.

  • There are even chances of creating fake aadhar cards.

  • It is very hard to find the difference between fake and real aadhar cards to an untrained person.

  • Tracking back is impossible if fake information is given.


SOLUTION


The only reason for services to ask for ID verification is to make sure that their service doesn't end up in the wrong hands.

Also, to track back if found so.

So, here comes our VERIF card.


  • Users’ privacy will be protected as he/she is not required to provide any information about him/her.

  • There is no misuse of information as no data is given to those service providers.

  • The decentralized server has no security compromises.

  • There is no chance of creating fakes as data provided is compared with data in the servers.

  • Service providers will get a message of authentication from the government servers.

  • Tracking back is easy as the information of use of the card is also stored.


CONCLUSION


Despite the fact that it is a large-scale project,we think it will be most effective and user friendly in all aspects and help both users/citizens and the government in the smooth functioning.
